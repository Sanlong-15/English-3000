# 3000 Common English Words

An offline study page for the 3,000 most common English words, built for Khmer speakers learning English.

**[Open the app](https://sanlong-15.github.io/English-3000/)**

---

## What it does

Every word has:

- a simple English definition
- a Khmer translation
- part of speech
- CEFR level (A1, A2, B1, B2)
- three example sentences, one at each of A1, A2 and B1 difficulty
- a speaker button that says the word out loud

## Features

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Search | By English word, meaning, or Khmer |
| Filter | By group, CEFR level, or word type — filters stack |
| Groups | 27 topic groups (Function words, Food & drink, Technology & repair, Phrasal verbs …) |
| Flashcards | Arrow keys to move, spacebar to flip, `S` to hear the word |
| Speech | Built-in browser voice, no audio files, works offline |
| Slow mode | Halves the speaking speed for difficult words |

## Contents

- **3,000 words** and **9,000 example sentences**
- **Levels:** 687 A1 · 718 A2 · 901 B1 · 694 B2
- **18 word types**, including 60 phrasal verbs
- One file, ~725 KB, no internet needed after loading

## How to use it

Open `index.html` in a browser. That is all — there is no build step, no install, and no server.

**Best browser:** Chrome or Edge. The speaking voice comes from your operating system, and Edge on Windows has the best English voices. Firefox works but sounds robotic.

## Suggested study method

1. Pick one group and one level, for example *Food & drink* + *A2*
2. Switch to Flashcards
3. Do 20 words a day

At 20 words a day the whole list takes about 150 days. That is the realistic pace — the limit is memory, not the file.

## Known limitations

- **Khmer translations** were written by an AI and checked by a human only in places. Some will be imperfect. Corrections welcome.
- **Three groups are loose.** *Common actions* (368), *Ideas & concepts* (337) and *Describing words* (316) are catch-all groups. Some words in them could sit in a better group.
- **Voice quality depends on your computer**, not on this file.
- The example sentences label the *sentence* difficulty, not the word. A B2 word still gets a simple A1 sentence first.
